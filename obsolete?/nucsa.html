CSA Cultural Showcase

February 2016

Graphic Design

This print was done for the Northeastern Chinese Student Association's first cultural showcase. Done in Illustrator, it features a vector-based dragon and a subtle background texture that mimics rice paper.

Design Process

Brainstorming

When brainstorming for this print, I considered the themes of the showcase. The dragon dance, performed by the executive board of CSA was one of the highlights and a common symbol in Chinese culture, so I used that as my main image. I also wanted to highlight our guest performer MC Jin.

Drafting

In the drafts of this print, I played around with the background gradient, typefaces, and the vectors behind the text. I began with a peachy red to red gradient, before trying tones that were more grey or more red. I tried to use scattered triangles with a low opacity behind the text to let it stand out from the strong background gradient, but the shapes became distracting.

Finalization

The final draft of this print uses bold red background with a gradual yet short gradient into a peachy, greyish color. To balance the complex shape of the dragon and boldness color of the background, I used a faint rectangle behind the text. I overlayed the dragon's claws on the logos at the bottom to better integrate them; I loved the detail it created.
