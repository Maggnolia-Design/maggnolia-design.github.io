Typography

Poster | Print, Digital

This section features some Chinese typographic exercises and an informative typography poster. In these Chinese typography exercises, I strongly correlated the forms of the characters with their meaning, emphasizing the pictoral foundation of written Chinese language.

Chinese Type Play

"半途而废": This typographic piece illustrates a Chinese saying which translates to "walking half the road and giving up." Here, I used half circles to replace pieces of the typographic forms to create a self-referential expression of this concept.

"川形": The pieces on the middle left translate to "river/plain" and "form." The vertical san serif text and red square connote East Asian calligraphy and traditional seals.

"霏雨閣": The piece beside it translates to "flying rain" and "pavillion." The small dashes and horizontal lines of the three characters (which indicate a word associated with water") are replaced with blue fields which represent rain. The yellow square is reminiscent of a door, awash in golden light.

Adobe Jenson Poster

This Adobe Jenson poster displays the distinguishing facts and features of the typeface. Attention to contrast, depth, color, opacity, and size were essential to creating tension and dynamic justapositions.
