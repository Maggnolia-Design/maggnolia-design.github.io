Peony Pavilion

The Peony Pavilion is a famous Kunqu opera from the Ming Dynasty. The poster's contemporary style pairs well with its highlighting of key visual elements distinctive to traditional Chinese opera. For example, Kunqu opera actors often draw out their facial features by using pink and black paint on a white face. They also dawn heavily ornamented headdresses and curls of hair on the forehead.

Tea House

Tea House is a 1957 play by Lao She. The play's three scenes represent three periods in Chinese history, each characterized by unique political and social problems.

The tea house becomes a microcosm of modern Chinese society as customers and others come to discuss contemporary issues. The poster's three pots and cups, as well as its auestere colors, represent the three scenes and three stages of struggle experienced in the tea house.
